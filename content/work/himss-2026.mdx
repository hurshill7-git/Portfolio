<Section eyebrow="01 · Context & problem" title="Design with real, unforgiving constraints">

A booth is experience design with rules that do not negotiate: the structure, the sightlines, where the TVs and cables go, what a large-format printer can actually produce, and a hanging sign with real physics. On top of that, the whole thing has to read as one campaign across the booth, the screens, and social.

<InPlainTerms>
HIMSS is one of the biggest health-tech conferences in the US. A booth there is a small, crowded storefront on a giant floor, and you get maybe a few seconds to make thousands of busy people decide to stop walking and come talk to you.
</InPlainTerms>

</Section>

<Section eyebrow="02 · Role & process" title="From 3D design to the loading dock">

I designed the booth in 3D (light and dark options, full dimensions). I wrote the production spec for the vendor: cable holes, grommets behind the TVs, print tolerances so the gradients would not band, and the frame for the hanging sign. I built the campaign around it (posters, a LinkedIn carousel, product and panel videos for the screens, social posts). And I handled the handoff: print-ready files and direct coordination with the manufacturer.

</Section>

<Section eyebrow="03 · Key decision" title="Designed for the physical medium first">

<Decision n="01" title="Built manufacturing into the design">

**What.** Designed the grommets, print tolerances, and sightlines in from the start, so the creative could actually be built.

**Why.** A gorgeous design means nothing if the printer cannot reproduce it or the structure will not fit through the door. On a show floor, "looks great on screen" is where the trouble begins, not ends.

**Tradeoff.** More detailed specs and more back-and-forth with the vendor.

**Result.** The booth shipped on time, on spec, and looked the way it was supposed to.

</Decision>

</Section>

<Section eyebrow="04 · Solution & artifacts" title="What shipped">

- A 3D booth design (light and dark options)
- A production spec for the vendor (cable holes, grommets, tolerances, frame specs)
- A campaign system (posters, LinkedIn carousel, product videos, TV assets, social posts)
- Print-ready file export and vendor coordination

<Section eyebrow="05 · Impact" title="Built and installed in Las Vegas">

The event ran in March 2026 in Las Vegas. The booth was built and installed to spec, and the LinkedIn campaign ran as part of 314e's broader conference push.

<Section eyebrow="06 · Reflection" title="A brand in the physical world">

HIMSS showed I can take a brand off the screen and into a room, and design it within constraints that genuinely do not bend. It is a useful counterweight to product work, where you can always just nudge a pixel.

</Section>
