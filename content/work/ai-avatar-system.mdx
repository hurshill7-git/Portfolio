<Section eyebrow="01 — Context" title="Explainers, at volume">

A SaaS product ships features constantly, and each needs a clear walkthrough.
Producing avatar-led explainer videos one at a time meant every render drifted —
different framing, pacing, and lower-thirds.

</Section>

<Section eyebrow="02 — What I built" title="A look, codified">

I defined the visual language for the avatars and their environment, then turned
it into a reusable template and a written guideline so anyone could produce a
video that still felt like one product.

- Framing, motion, and typography rules for lower-thirds and callouts
- A reusable scene/template kit
- A short guideline doc covering do/don&apos;t and tone

</Section>

<Section eyebrow="03 — Outcome" title="Consistent by default">

New explainers now start from the template, so consistency is the default rather
than a review step. The brand reads the same across the whole library.

</Section>
