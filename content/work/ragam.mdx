<Section eyebrow="01 — Context" title="A festival that outgrew its programme">

Ragam runs a packed calendar of cultural events — performances, workshops, and
competitions across multiple venues and days. The information existed, but it
lived in PDFs and posts. Attendees couldn&apos;t see what was on *now*, what clashed,
or how to get a seat.

<Callout>
The job wasn&apos;t to list events. It was to help someone standing in a crowd decide
where to go next — in seconds.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section eyebrow="02 — My role" title="Where I focused">

Working alongside another designer, I owned the discovery and scheduling
experience and co-built the UI system. I led the mobile-first interaction model
and the live-schedule view.

</Section>

<Section eyebrow="03 — Approach" title="Plan-your-day, not browse-a-catalog">

We reframed the product around a personal schedule. You browse by interest,
save what you want, and the app builds your day — flagging clashes and travel
time between venues. Ticketing folds into the same flow so there&apos;s no detour.

</Section>

<Section eyebrow="04 — Design" title="Warm, energetic, legible">

The brand needed to feel festive without becoming noise. We anchored on a warm
palette, generous type, and strong photography, then kept the interface itself
quiet so content could sing.
