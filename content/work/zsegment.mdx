<Section eyebrow="01 — Context" title="A powerful tool only experts could drive">

ZSegment lets healthcare organizations slice patient populations by hundreds of
clinical and demographic criteria — the engine behind care-gap programs, outreach
campaigns, and risk analysis. The logic was best-in-class. The experience was not.

Analysts built segments by stacking raw query conditions with little feedback on
what they were doing or whether it was right. Onboarding took a week of
hand-holding, and even seasoned users second-guessed their results.

<Callout>
The product wasn&apos;t missing features. It was missing a mental model — a way to
*see* the population narrowing as you reasoned about it.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section eyebrow="02 — My role" title="What I owned">

I led design end-to-end: discovery interviews, the IA rework, interaction model,
the visual system, and the working relationship with PM and engineering. I ran
weekly critiques and paired with engineers through build to protect the details.

- Framed the problem and aligned the team on success metrics
- Designed the segmentation builder, results view, and component library
- Established the design tokens and patterns the wider product now reuses

</Section>

<Section eyebrow="03 — Research" title="Where people got stuck">

I shadowed five analysts and mapped every step of building a real segment. Three
patterns showed up again and again.

1. **No sense of progress.** Adding a condition gave no feedback on population size.
2. **Fear of mistakes.** Irreversible actions and cryptic errors made people cautious.
3. **Expert-only language.** Labels assumed deep domain fluency new hires didn&apos;t have.

<Section eyebrow="04 — Explorations" title="Making the population visible">

The core bet: turn an abstract query into a living, narrowing population you can
watch respond to every decision. I explored three directions — a wizard, a
canvas, and an inline builder with a persistent live count — and tested low-fi
versions with analysts.

The inline builder won. It kept power users fast while giving newcomers
continuous feedback and safe, reversible edits.

<Section eyebrow="05 — Solution" title="A guided builder with a live population">

Every condition now updates a live count and distribution. Plain-language
summaries restate the logic in a sentence. Destructive actions are reversible, and
the empty state teaches the model instead of staring back blankly.

<Section eyebrow="07 — Reflection" title="What I&apos;d carry forward">

The unlock wasn&apos;t a feature; it was feedback. Once people could *see* the
population respond, confidence followed. If I revisited this, I&apos;d invest next in
saved segment templates and a lightweight audit trail for compliance reviews.
